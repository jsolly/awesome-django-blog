My Bio

As a geospatial software engineer, I combine GIS domain knowledge and full-stack development to create beautiful (and fast) mapping applications.

I studied GIS under thought leaders Michael Goodchild and Waldo Tobler while completing a bachelor's in Geography at UCSB and an M.S. in Geoinformatics and Geospatial Intelligence at George Mason University. Upon graduation, I joined Esri as a product engineer to launch ArcGIS Dashboards out of beta to become one of the world's most viewed geospatial web applications.

I've always been 'technical,' writing scripts to automate myself out of jobs, but things got serious when I began an open-source CS degree equivalent through Open Source Society University. Part-way through the program, I accepted a position at UMD, where I work today as a technical lead and geospatial software engineer funded through a grant from the Filecoin Foundation for the Decentralized Web. Learn more about the project at easierdata.org

If you have complex geospatial challenges that require a developer to solve, Drop me a message, and let's explore our fit together!
